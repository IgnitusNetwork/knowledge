# Self-Assessment: Which Model Am I Dealing With?

## Why this matters
Many systems look similar on the surface (they offer yields from trading activity), but they work in very different ways. Knowing whether you are in a **Counterparty Model** or a **Pure Backed Asset Model** helps you understand where your money is really at risk.

## Quick Self-Assessment Questionnaire
Answer these 8 questions honestly. Score 1 point for each “Yes” answer that points to counterparty risk.

1. Does the system require me to provide liquidity that is used as the opposite side of leveraged trades?  
2. Can traders win money directly from the pool or vault that holds my funds?  
3. If traders as a group make big profits, will my share of the pool lose value?  
4. Are the advertised yields mainly from trading fees while the pool also bears trading losses?  
5. Is my money mixed with other users’ funds in a shared pool that pays out winning traders?  
6. Does the system have liquidation mechanisms or funding rates that can transfer value between users?  
7. Can sophisticated traders or large players systematically extract value from the pool?  
8. Is it possible for the pool value to drop even when trading volume is high?

**Scoring**  
- **0–2 Yes answers**: You are likely in a Pure Backed Asset Model (or very low counterparty exposure).  
- **3–5 Yes answers**: Mixed or partial counterparty risk — understand the exact mechanics.  
- **6+ Yes answers**: You are probably in a Counterparty Model. Your funds are directly exposed to trader performance.

## What each model means for your money

**Pure Backed Asset Model**  
Your deposit sits in an isolated vault holding only the real asset (e.g. dollars, gold, bitcoin). Trading fees are added to strengthen the backing. Volume can only help — never hurt — the value per token. No trader wins at your expense.

**Counterparty Model**  
Your money is part of a shared pool that acts as the opponent to leveraged traders. When traders win, their profits come out of the pool. Fees may help, but they do not always offset losses from winning traders.

## Safety checklist (before you put money in)
- Can I clearly see and verify exactly where my funds are held?  
- Does the system ever use my deposit to pay other users’ trading profits?  
- What is the worst-case scenario for my funds if traders are very successful?  
- Have I read the rules on how fees are used and who benefits?  
- Am I comfortable with the level of risk this model creates?  
- Do I fully understand how I can get my money back?

## Recommendation
If you scored high on counterparty risk and prefer capital preservation over high advertised yields, look for systems that use a Pure Backed Asset Model. Always start with very small amounts and verify everything yourself on-chain.
