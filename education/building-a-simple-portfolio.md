# Building a Simple Portfolio

## Why this matters
A basic mix of assets can reduce risk compared to putting everything in one place.

## Step-by-step guide
1. Decide your risk tolerance and time horizon.  
2. Split your money: e.g. 40% safe savings, 30% stable assets, 20% growth assets, 10% higher-risk (only money you can lose).  
3. Choose 4–8 different things instead of one.  
4. Re-balance once or twice a year.  
5. Keep good records of what you own.

## Common pitfalls
Putting everything in the latest hot asset. Never checking or re-balancing. Using money needed soon for long-term holdings.

## Safety checklist
- Is any single asset more than 20–25% of the total?  
- Do I understand each part of my portfolio?  
- Can I access enough cash quickly if needed?
