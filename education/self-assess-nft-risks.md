# Self-Assessment: NFT Marketplace & Collection Risks

## Why this matters
NFTs can look simple (just buy and sell digital art or items), but they come with hidden risks most users don’t see until it’s too late. This short questionnaire helps you understand what you are really exposed to.

## Quick Self-Assessment Questionnaire
Answer “Yes” or “No”. Score 1 point for each “Yes”.

1. Am I buying an NFT from a collection where the smart contract can still be changed by the team (upgradeable or has admin keys)?  
2. Does the marketplace or collection have a history of changing royalty rules after launch?  
3. Am I relying on the team’s promises for future utility or value instead of what is already on-chain?  
4. Is the NFT locked in a contract I cannot easily verify or withdraw from?  
5. Does the project use off-chain metadata (hosted on a normal website) that could disappear?  
6. Am I buying at a price far above the floor because of FOMO or influencer hype?  
7. Is the liquidity very low, so selling later might be difficult without big price drops?  
8. Have I not checked the contract address myself on Etherscan/Solscan?

**Scoring**  
- **0–2 Yes**: Lower immediate risk — still verify everything.  
- **3–5 Yes**: Medium risk — proceed with extra caution and small amounts only.  
- **6+ Yes**: High risk — strong chance of loss from contract changes, rug, or illiquidity.

## What this means for your money
NFTs are not just pictures. You are buying a token whose value depends on the smart contract rules, the team’s honesty, marketplace mechanics, and real demand. Many users lose money because they only look at the art and price, not the underlying risks.

## Safety checklist before buying any NFT
- Verify the exact contract address from official sources.  
- Check if the contract is upgradeable or has admin functions.  
- Confirm metadata is on-chain or on permanent storage (IPFS/Arweave).  
- Understand the royalty enforcement (if any).  
- Test with a very small purchase first.  
- Ask: “If the team disappeared tomorrow, what would this NFT still be worth?”
