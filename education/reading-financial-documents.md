# Reading Financial Documents

## Why this matters
Documents contain the real rules — not the marketing claims.

## Step-by-step guide
1. Start with the summary or “key facts” section.  
2. Look for sections on risks, fees, and what can go wrong.  
3. Search for words like “may”, “could”, “risk”, and “loss”.  
4. Check numbers: fees, past performance, guarantees (if any).  
5. Ask: What does this mean for my money in plain English?

## Common pitfalls
Skipping to the returns section. Believing glossy brochures over the legal text. Not reading updates.

## Safety checklist
- Can I explain the main risks in my own words?  
- Are all fees clearly listed?  
- Have I read the latest version?
