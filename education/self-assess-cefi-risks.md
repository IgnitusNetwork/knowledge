# Self-Assessment: CeFi Yield / Platform Risks

## Why this matters
CeFi platforms offer convenient yields and staking, but you give up control of your assets to a centralized company. This questionnaire helps you quickly evaluate how much counterparty and platform risk you are taking on.

## Quick Self-Assessment Questionnaire
Answer “Yes” or “No”. Score 1 point for each “Yes”.

1. Am I depositing assets into a platform where I do not hold the private keys?  
2. Would I become an unsecured creditor if the platform goes bankrupt?  
3. Can the platform change yields, lock funds, or suspend withdrawals at its discretion?  
4. Does the platform re-use (rehypothecate) my assets for its own lending or trading?  
5. Is there no real-time, verifiable on-chain proof of reserves for my specific assets?  
6. Have I not read the latest risk disclosures and terms of service?  
7. Am I relying on high advertised yields without understanding where they actually come from?  
8. Would a regulatory action or platform decision in one country affect my ability to withdraw?

**Scoring**  
- **0–2 Yes**: Lower immediate CeFi-specific risk — still verify custody and terms.  
- **3–5 Yes**: Medium risk — you are exposed to meaningful counterparty and platform discretion.  
- **6+ Yes**: High risk — strong potential for loss of access or partial recovery in a platform failure.

## What this means for your money
In CeFi, you trade self-custody for convenience. If the platform fails, gets hacked, or faces regulatory action, you can lose access to your funds for months or years — and you may recover only a portion (as seen in past cases). Yields often include a hidden “custody premium” that reduces your real return.

## Safety checklist before using any CeFi yield product
- Read the full risk disclosures and terms of service.  
- Confirm whether you hold the keys or the platform does.  
- Check if they publish verifiable proof-of-reserves (and understand its limitations).  
- Start with a very small amount and test withdrawals.  
- Ask: “What happens to my assets if the company goes bankrupt or is forced to stop operations?”  
- Diversify across multiple platforms and self-custody options.  
- Only use money you can afford to lose access to for an extended period.
