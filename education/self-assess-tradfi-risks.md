# Self-Assessment: TradFi Risks (Hedge Funds, Portfolio Managers, Retirement Plans)

## Why this matters
Traditional finance often feels “safe” because of regulation and institutions, but layered fees, custody chains, and structural conflicts can quietly erode your money. This questionnaire helps you see the hidden risks before you invest.

## Quick Self-Assessment Questionnaire
Answer “Yes” or “No”. Score 1 point for each “Yes”.

1. Am I paying fees at multiple layers (e.g., feeder fund + master fund + portfolio manager)?  
2. Is my capital in a structure where the manager earns performance fees even if I lose money overall?  
3. Do I have limited or no direct control over where my money is actually traded or custodied?  
4. Are my assets subject to rehypothecation or reuse by a prime broker or central entity?  
5. Would a failure at the broker or custodian level leave me as an unsecured creditor?  
6. Am I in a retirement plan (401(k), pension) where the plan sponsor chooses high-fee alternatives with gated liquidity?  
7. Do I face exit fees, redemption gates, or new high-water marks if I switch managers?  
8. Have I not reviewed the latest fee schedule, partnership agreement, or ERISA fiduciary disclosures?

**Scoring**  
- **0–2 Yes**: Lower layered-risk exposure — still verify custody and fees.  
- **3–5 Yes**: Medium risk — layered fees and custody chains are likely eroding returns.  
- **6+ Yes**: High risk — significant potential for fee drag, illiquidity, or loss of control.

## What this means for your money
TradFi often adds fees and intermediaries at every layer. Even “sophisticated” structures can result in 3–4%+ annual drag plus custody risks that leave you exposed in a crisis. Regulation protects the system more than it protects you as an individual investor.

## Safety checklist before investing in TradFi structures
- Request the full fee waterfall (every layer) in writing.  
- Confirm who holds legal custody and whether assets can be rehypothecated.  
- Ask: “If the prime broker or custodian fails, what happens to my capital?”  
- Review high-water marks, gates, and lock-up periods.  
- For retirement plans: check the fiduciary’s documented process for selecting alternatives.  
- Start with the smallest possible allocation and monitor net returns after all fees.
