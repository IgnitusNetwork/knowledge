# Self-Assessment: DeFi Insurance / Coverage Risks

## Why this matters
Insurance in DeFi promises protection, but many products have hidden limitations. This checklist helps you understand what you are actually buying.

## Quick Self-Assessment Questionnaire
Score 1 point for each “Yes”.

1. Does the insurance require me to wait for a governance vote or committee decision before a claim is paid?  
2. Is the coverage pool under-capitalized compared to the total possible claims?  
3. Can the protocol change claim rules or deny coverage after I have paid the premium?  
4. Is the insurance triggered only by very specific on-chain events (parametric) that might miss real losses?  
5. Am I relying on a single team or small group to manage the capital pool?  
6. Does the insurance have a cap on payout (e.g. maximum $X per event)?  
7. Have I not verified the audit status and contract addresses myself?  
8. Is the premium very high relative to the coverage amount?

**Scoring**  
- **0–2 Yes**: Relatively cleaner structure — still read the full policy.  
- **3–5 Yes**: Medium risk — understand exactly what is covered and what is excluded.  
- **6+ Yes**: High risk of claim denial or insufficient payout.

## What this means for your money
DeFi insurance is not like traditional insurance. Many products use pooled capital, voting mechanisms, or strict parametric triggers. This can lead to delays, disputes, or partial/no payouts even when a real loss occurs.

## Safety checklist before buying coverage
- Read the exact policy terms and exclusions.  
- Check the current capital pool size vs maximum possible claims.  
- Verify the claims process is on-chain and transparent.  
- Confirm the contracts are audited and immutable where possible.  
- Start with small coverage amounts to test the process.  
- Ask: “What happens in a real exploit — who decides and how fast do I get paid?”
