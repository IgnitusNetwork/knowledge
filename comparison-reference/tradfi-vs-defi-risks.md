# TradFi vs DeFi Risks — Structural Comparison (User POV)

## Core Difference in Design
**TradFi**: Capital flows through multiple trusted intermediaries (banks, brokers, funds, recordkeepers). Regulation and fiduciary duties are meant to protect the system.  
**DeFi**: Capital is held directly by users or in transparent on-chain vaults/smart contracts. No intermediary — rules are code.

## Key Risk Categories (User Perspective)

**Custody & Asset Control**  
TradFi: Assets held by institutions or prime brokers. You are an unsecured creditor if the custodian fails. Rehypothecation is common.  
DeFi: Self-custody or on-chain vaults. You control the keys, but a smart-contract bug can mean permanent loss.

**Fee Layers & Erosion**  
TradFi: Multiple layers (feeder → master → sub-advisers → recordkeepers) plus management + performance fees. Total drag often 2–4%+ annually.  
DeFi: Protocol fees are usually transparent and single-layer, but some models add hidden slippage, liquidation costs, or gas.

**Liquidity & Access**  
TradFi: Daily liquidity for retail accounts, but hedge funds/alts often have gates, lock-ups, or redemption queues.  
DeFi: Instant on-chain transfers, but some pools can become illiquid during stress.

**Liability & Recourse**  
TradFi: Limited liability for investors, but managers have fiduciary duties. In practice, you bear losses while managers collect fees.  
DeFi: No fiduciary — code is law. Losses from exploits or design flaws are final unless the community forks or compensates.

**Systemic vs Technical Risk**  
TradFi: Slow-moving systemic risks (bank failures, inflation, regulatory changes). Prime-broker failures can freeze assets for long periods.  
DeFi: Fast-moving technical risks (smart-contract exploits, oracle failures, liquidity cascades). Losses are often concentrated but can spread via composability.

**Regulation & Protection**  
TradFi: Heavy regulation and insurance schemes (e.g., SIPC, FDIC) for certain accounts, but not for hedge funds or many alternative structures.  
DeFi: Minimal regulation — user responsibility is total. No safety net if code fails.

## Bottom Line for Your Money
TradFi risks are often hidden in layers and intermediaries — you pay for “professional management” that may not align with your interests.  
DeFi risks are transparent but unforgiving — you control the assets, but you also bear full responsibility for code and market mechanics.

**Safety checklist (any system)**  
- Who really controls my assets?  
- How many fee layers exist?  
- What happens in a crisis — can I access my money?  
- Who bears the losses if something breaks?  
- Have I verified the rules (contracts or legal docs) myself?
