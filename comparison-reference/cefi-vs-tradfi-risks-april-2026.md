# CeFi vs TradFi Risks Knowledge Base (April 2026 Snapshot)

This is a fact-based comparison drawn exclusively from official platform disclosures, regulatory filings (SEC/DOL/DFPI), bankruptcy records, and 2026 analyses. It directly contrasts CeFi yield products with TradFi structures from prior databases. Facts only—no opinions.

### Core Mechanisms
**CeFi Yield Products** (e.g., Binance Earn, Coinbase Staking, Kraken Auto Earn, Nexo):  
Platforms take full custodial control of user assets. Yields are paid from internal lending, staking (via platform validators), or proprietary strategies. Users are unsecured creditors; assets may be re-used internally. No on-chain transparency into yield generation.

**TradFi Structures** (hedge funds via master-feeder, prime brokerage, ERISA retirement plans):  
Capital flows through layered vehicles (investor → feeder → master fund → prime broker). Prime brokers provide custody, financing, and execution for multiple hedge funds. Retirement plans add recordkeeper and fiduciary layers. Assets are often rehypothecated.

### Key Structural Parallels
- **Central Intermediary Control**: Both rely on a trusted third party that collects revenue while end users bear the downside.  
- **Rehypothecation / Asset Reuse**: Common in both (prime brokers in TradFi; internal lending/staking in CeFi).  
- **Unsecured Creditor Status**: In failure, users/LPs rank behind secured creditors.  
- **Yield vs Alpha Drag**: High headline yields or fees in both often mask custody and intermediary risks.  
- **No Direct Control**: Users do not hold keys (CeFi) or direct master-fund positions (TradFi feeders).
