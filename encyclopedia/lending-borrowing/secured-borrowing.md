# Secured Borrowing (Loans Backed by Something You Own)

## What it is
Borrowing money where you offer something valuable (like a house or car) as security. If you cannot repay, the lender can take that item.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
You get a lower interest rate because the lender has protection. You keep using the item (house or car) while repaying. If payments stop, you risk losing the item.

## Common ways users get hurt
Missing payments can lead to losing your home or car. Even if the item is worth more than the loan, selling it in a hurry often means you still lose money.

## Simple example with numbers
You borrow $200,000 against your house.  
You pay $1,200 per month.  
If you miss many payments, the lender can sell the house to recover their money. You may end up with nothing left after costs.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- What exactly can the lender take if I cannot pay?  
- How much of my monthly payment reduces the actual loan?  
- Can I afford the payments if my income drops?
