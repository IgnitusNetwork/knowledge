# Interest Rates Explained (Simple Terms)

## What it is
The extra percentage you pay when borrowing or earn when lending.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
A 5% rate on $10,000 means $500 extra per year. Small differences in rates add up to thousands of dollars over time.

## Common ways users get hurt
Compounding interest on loans makes debt grow faster than you expect. Low rates on savings barely keep up with rising prices.

## Simple example with numbers
$10,000 loan at 5% = $500 extra per year.  
$10,000 loan at 15% = $1,500 extra per year.  
The higher rate costs you three times more.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- Is the rate fixed or can it change?  
- How is interest calculated (daily, monthly, yearly)?  
- What is the total cost over the full period?
