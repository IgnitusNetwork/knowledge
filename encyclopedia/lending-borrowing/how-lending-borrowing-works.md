# How Lending and Borrowing Works

## What it is
Lending is giving your money to someone else (or an institution) so they can use it and pay you extra later. Borrowing is the opposite — taking money now and promising to pay it back with extra.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
When you lend, your money is used by the borrower for their needs. You get interest as reward. When you borrow, you get money now but must repay more later. The lender earns the difference.

## Common ways users get hurt
Borrowers pay much more than they receive. Lenders can lose money if the borrower cannot repay. Both sides face the risk that the agreement goes wrong.

## Simple example with numbers
You lend $5,000 for one year at 8% interest.  
You receive $5,400 back.  
The borrower pays the extra $400 for using your money.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- How likely is it that I will get my money back?  
- What happens if the borrower cannot pay?  
- What are all the costs and fees involved?
