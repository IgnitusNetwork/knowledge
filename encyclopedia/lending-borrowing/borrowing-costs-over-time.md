# Borrowing Costs Over Time

## What it is
The total real cost of borrowing when you look at many years instead of just monthly payments.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
A loan that looks affordable each month can cost you double or more over 10–30 years because of interest.

## Common ways users get hurt
People focus only on the monthly payment and underestimate the total amount paid.

## Simple example with numbers
A $200,000 loan over 30 years at 6%.  
Monthly payment ≈ $1,200.  
Total paid ≈ $432,000.  
Interest cost = $232,000 — more than the original loan.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- What is the total repayment amount (not just monthly)?  
- How many years will I be paying?  
- Can I pay it off faster to reduce the total cost?
