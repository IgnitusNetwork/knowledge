# Liquidation and Default

## What it is
Default is when a borrower stops making payments. Liquidation is when the lender sells the borrower’s assets to recover money.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
As a borrower you can lose the items you put up as security. As a lender you may still lose part of your money if the sold assets are not enough.

## Common ways users get hurt
Forced sales happen at bad prices, so even secured lenders lose money. Borrowers lose their assets and still owe remaining debt in some cases.

## Simple example with numbers
You borrow $80,000 against an asset worth $100,000.  
Asset value drops to $70,000.  
The lender sells it and you still owe $10,000 plus fees.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- How much can the asset value drop before liquidation?  
- What are the exact rules and timelines?  
- Do I have any protection or warning period?
