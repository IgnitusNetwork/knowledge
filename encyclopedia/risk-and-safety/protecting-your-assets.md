# Protecting Your Assets

## What it is
Practical steps to reduce the chance of losing your money through theft, hacks, mistakes, or system failures.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
You spread and secure your savings so that a single problem (lost phone, hacked account, or bigger failure) does not wipe out everything you have worked for.

## Common ways users get hurt
Keeping everything in one place or one account. Using weak passwords. Clicking suspicious links. Not having simple backups.

## Simple example with numbers
You have $50,000 total savings.  
$40,000 in one account gets frozen or hacked.  
You lose access to most of your money for weeks or months.  
If you had split it across different places, you would still have $10,000–$20,000 available immediately.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- Do I have my money in more than one place?  
- Do I use strong, unique passwords and two-factor authentication?  
- Have I written down (offline) how to access my accounts?  
- Do I regularly check statements for anything unusual?  
- Do I understand who would protect my money if the institution fails?
