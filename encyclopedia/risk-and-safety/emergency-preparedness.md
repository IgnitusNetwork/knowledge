# Emergency Preparedness for Your Money

## What it is
Having plans and reserves ready so that unexpected life events (job loss, medical bills, car breakdown) do not force you into bad financial decisions.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
You keep some easily accessible cash or safe savings that you can use quickly without selling investments at the wrong time or taking expensive loans.

## Common ways users get hurt
No emergency fund → forced to sell assets during a market drop or borrow at high interest. Panic decisions made under stress usually cost more.

## Simple example with numbers
You lose your job and need $3,000 quickly for rent and bills.  
If you have an emergency fund, you use it calmly.  
Without it, you sell investments at a 25% loss or take a high-interest loan.  
The extra cost can be $750–$1,500 or more.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- Do I have 3–6 months of basic living expenses easily available?  
- Is that money in a safe and quick-to-access place?  
- Do I have a simple written plan for job loss or big unexpected costs?  
- Have I practiced not touching the emergency money for normal spending?
