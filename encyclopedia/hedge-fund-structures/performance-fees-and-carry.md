# Performance Fees and Carry

## What it is
A share of the profits that the fund managers take as their reward for good performance.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
If the fund makes money, the manager takes a big percentage (often 20%) of those gains before your share is calculated.

## Common ways users get hurt
You pay for gains, but the manager does not share the losses. This can encourage riskier behaviour.

## Simple example with numbers
Fund makes $200,000 profit on your investment.  
Manager takes 20% ($40,000).  
You receive $160,000 of the profit.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- Is there a minimum profit level before fees apply?  
- Does the manager only get paid on new profits?  
- How often are these fees taken?
