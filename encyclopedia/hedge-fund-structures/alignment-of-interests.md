# Alignment of Interests

## What it is
How well the goals of the fund managers match the goals of the investors.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
Good alignment means managers only do well when you do well. Poor alignment means they can earn large fees even when your investment suffers.

## Common ways users get hurt
Managers earn steady fees while your capital is at risk. This can lead to decisions that benefit them more than you.

## Simple example with numbers
Manager earns 2% fee every year ($200k on $10M) even if the fund loses money.  
They only share upside on big wins.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- Do the managers have a large part of their own money in the fund?  
- Are fees truly tied to long-term performance?  
- Are there clear rules that protect investors?
