# High Water Marks

## What it is
A rule that says the manager can only take performance fees after recovering all previous losses.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
If the fund loses money, the manager must bring it back above the previous highest value before they can charge performance fees again.

## Common ways users get hurt
Without this rule, managers could keep charging fees on recovering from their own mistakes. Even with it, recovering from big losses can take many years.

## Simple example with numbers
Your investment drops from $1M to $800k.  
The fund must first get back above $1M before the manager earns performance fees again.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- Does the fund have a high water mark rule?  
- How long has it been since the last high point?  
- Is it properly calculated and transparent?
