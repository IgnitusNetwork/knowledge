# Layer 2: Slippage and Spreads

## What it is
The difference between the price you expect and the price you actually get when buying or selling.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
In busy or illiquid markets you pay a little more when buying and receive a little less when selling. The gap is invisible until you check the final numbers.

## Common ways users get hurt
Large orders or fast-moving markets widen the gap. Repeated small losses add up quickly.

## Simple example with numbers
You want to buy at $100.  
You actually pay $101.50 because of slippage/spread.  
You sell at $110 but only receive $108.50.  
Extra cost on round-trip: $3.50 on a $100 position.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- What is the typical spread for the size I usually trade?  
- How volatile is the asset?  
- Can I place smaller orders to reduce slippage?
