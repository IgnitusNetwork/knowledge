# Layer 5: Impermanent Loss and Opportunity Cost

## What it is
Losses that happen when you lock money into one strategy while missing better or safer options elsewhere.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
Your money is committed, so you cannot use it when better opportunities appear. Sometimes the position itself loses relative value even if the price does not drop.

## Common ways users get hurt
You stay locked in while prices move in ways that hurt your specific position. You also miss simple holding or other uses for the capital.

## Simple example with numbers
You commit $10,000 to a paired position.  
One asset rises 50%, the other stays flat.  
You end up with less total value than if you had just held the rising asset alone.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- How long is my money committed?  
- What could I do with this money instead?  
- How does the position perform in different market scenarios?
