# Layer 4: Management and Performance Fees

## What it is
Ongoing charges for managing money plus a share of any profits made.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
A fixed fee is taken every year no matter what. An extra cut is taken when there are gains. These fees continue even during flat or slightly down periods.

## Common ways users get hurt
Fees reduce compounding. Over 10+ years they can take 30–50% or more of potential growth.

## Simple example with numbers
$100,000 investment.  
2% management fee = $2,000 per year.  
20% performance fee on gains.  
After 10 years the fees can easily exceed $30,000–$50,000.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- What are the exact percentages and how are they calculated?  
- Do fees apply even when returns are low?  
- Is there a high-water mark?
