# Credit Cards

## What it is
A plastic card (or phone tap) that lets you spend money you don’t have yet. The bank pays the shop and you pay the bank later.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
You buy things during the month. If you pay the full balance by the due date, you usually pay no extra. If you only pay part, the remaining balance starts collecting high interest every month.

## Common ways users get hurt
- Carrying a balance leads to very high interest that grows fast.  
- Late fees and cash advance fees add up quickly.  
- Easy spending makes people buy more than they can afford.

## Simple example with numbers
You spend $1,200 on your card in a month.  
You pay only $200 on time.  
The remaining $1,000 now charges 22% interest per year.  
After one year of minimum payments you could still owe more than you originally spent.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- What is the interest rate if I don’t pay in full?  
- What is the minimum payment and when is it due?  
- Are there annual fees or foreign transaction fees?  
- Do I have a clear limit I can actually afford?
