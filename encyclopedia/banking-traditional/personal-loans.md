# Personal Loans

## What it is
A loan from the bank where you borrow a lump sum of money and pay it back over time with extra interest added.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
You get the cash now (for a car, home repairs, medical bills, etc.). Every month you pay back part of the loan plus interest. The total you repay is more than you borrowed. The bank takes the payments automatically from your account.

## Common ways users get hurt
- Interest rates are high, so you end up paying back a lot more than you received.  
- Late payments add big extra fees and can damage your ability to borrow again.  
- Borrowing for things you don’t really need makes life more expensive long-term.

## Simple example with numbers
You borrow $10,000 for a car.  
Interest rate 9% over 5 years.  
You pay back about $12,700 in total.  
The extra $2,700 is what the loan actually cost you.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- What is the full interest rate and total amount I will repay?  
- Are there any early repayment penalties?  
- Can I afford the monthly payment even if my income drops?  
- Is the loan secured against something I own?
