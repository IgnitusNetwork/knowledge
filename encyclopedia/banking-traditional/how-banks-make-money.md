# How Banks Make Money (User Perspective)

## What it is
Banks are businesses. They earn profit by using the money people deposit.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
When you put money in a savings or checking account, the bank does not keep it in a safe doing nothing. They lend most of it to other customers (home loans, car loans, business loans) at a much higher rate than they pay you. The difference is their main profit.

## Common ways users get hurt
You earn almost nothing on your savings while the bank earns a lot more on the same money. Over decades this gap quietly moves wealth from everyday savers to the bank and its borrowers.

## Simple example with numbers
You earn 0.5% on your savings.  
The bank lends that same money at 6–8% to someone buying a house.  
The bank keeps the big difference. Your small return barely beats nothing.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- How much interest am I really earning versus what borrowers pay?  
- Are my deposits safe if the bank lends too much and things go wrong?  
- Is there a government protection scheme and what is the limit?
