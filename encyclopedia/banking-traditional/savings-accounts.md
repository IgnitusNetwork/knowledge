# Savings Accounts

## What it is
A place at the bank where you put money you do not need right away. The bank pays you a small amount of extra money (called interest) for keeping it there.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
You deposit your salary or savings. The bank uses that money to lend to other people or businesses. In return, they give you a tiny percentage back each year. You can usually take your money out when you want, but sometimes there are limits on how many times per month.

## Common ways users get hurt
- The interest you earn is often lower than the rise in living costs (inflation), so your money slowly buys less over time.  
- Some accounts have monthly fees that eat into small balances.  
- If you leave the money too long without checking, you might miss better options.

## Simple example with numbers
You put $5,000 in a savings account.  
The bank pays 0.5% per year.  
After one year you have about $5,025 (before tax).  
Meanwhile, everyday prices went up 3%, so your $5,025 now buys less than $5,000 did last year.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- What is the actual interest rate right now?  
- Are there any monthly maintenance fees?  
- How easy is it to move money out when I need it?  
- Does the bank protect my deposit if something goes wrong with them?
