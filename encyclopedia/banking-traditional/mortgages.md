# Mortgages (Home Loans)

## What it is
A large, long-term loan used to buy a house or apartment. The house itself is used as security for the loan.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
You make a down payment, then pay the bank every month for 15–30 years. Part of each payment reduces the loan, the rest is interest. You get to live in the house while paying it off.

## Common ways users get hurt
- Small changes in interest rates or life circumstances can make payments unaffordable.  
- Over decades you pay far more in interest than the original house price.  
- If you cannot pay, the bank can take the house.

## Simple example with numbers
You buy a $300,000 house with $60,000 down.  
You borrow $240,000 over 30 years at 6%.  
Total paid back ≈ $517,000.  
You paid roughly $277,000 in interest alone.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- What is the total interest I will pay over the full term?  
- Can I still afford payments if interest rates rise or I lose income?  
- What are all the extra costs (insurance, taxes, fees)?  
- How much of my early payments actually reduce the loan?
