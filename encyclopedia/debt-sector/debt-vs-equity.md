# Debt versus Equity (Owning vs Borrowing)

## What it is
Debt = borrowing money you must repay. Equity = owning part of something (shares in a company).

## What happens to your money (user POV)
With debt you have fixed payments and must repay even if things go badly. With equity you share in the upside but can lose everything if it fails.

## Common ways users get hurt
Using too much debt increases risk of total loss during bad times. Relying only on equity can mean missing steady income.

## Simple example with numbers
Debt: Borrow $100k, must repay $120k no matter what.  
Equity: Invest $100k in a business — it may grow to $200k or drop to $0.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- How much of my total position is debt versus ownership?  
- Can I handle the fixed payments if income drops?  
- Do I understand the full risk of losing the money?
