# Corporate Debt

## What it is
Money that large companies borrow to grow their business, buy equipment, or expand.

## What happens to your money (user POV)
Companies use debt to operate or grow. If they do well, they repay with profit. If they struggle, they may cut jobs, raise prices, or even shut down — affecting employees and customers.

## Common ways users get hurt
When companies carry too much debt, they become fragile. In tough times they fire staff or go out of business, hurting ordinary workers and communities.

## Simple example with numbers
A company borrows $500 million to build new factories.  
If sales are good, they repay easily.  
If sales drop, they may lay off workers to save money for debt payments.

## What to check (safety checklist)
- How much debt does the company already have?  
- Is the debt used for real growth or just to cover losses?  
- What happens to workers and customers if the company struggles?
