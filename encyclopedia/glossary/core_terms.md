# Core Terms

## Asset
**Plain definition**  
Anything that has value and can be owned (money, property, digital tokens, etc.).

**Common confusion**  
Thinking that all assets are safe or easy to sell.

**How it shows up in real life**  
Your house, car, bank balance, or digital tokens are all assets.

**Safety note**  
Always ask: How easily can I turn this asset into cash if I need it urgently?

---

## Backing
**Plain definition**  
Real assets or money set aside to support the value of something else (for example, a token).

**Common confusion**  
Assuming that “backed” always means 100% safe and instantly redeemable.

**How it shows up in real life**  
A system says each token is supported by an equal amount of dollars or gold held somewhere.

**Safety note**  
Check the backing addresses and rules yourself regularly.

---

## Collateral
**Plain definition**  
Something valuable you offer as security when borrowing money.

**Common confusion**  
Believing you cannot lose the collateral if something goes wrong.

**How it shows up in real life**  
You use your car as collateral for a loan. If you stop paying, the lender can take the car.

**Safety note**  
Only borrow against collateral you are willing to lose.

---

## Compounding
**Plain definition**  
When your earnings start generating their own earnings over time.

**Common confusion**  
Underestimating how powerful small regular gains become over many years.

**How it shows up in real life**  
$1,000 earning 7% per year grows to about $1,967 after 10 years because of compounding.

**Safety note**  
Compounding works both ways — debt compounds against you too.

---

## Diversification
**Plain definition**  
Spreading your money across different types of investments.

**Common confusion**  
Thinking that owning five similar things counts as diversification.

**How it shows up in real life**  
Instead of putting all savings in one bank account or one asset, you split it between different options.

**Safety note**  
True diversification reduces the chance that one bad event wipes out most of your money.

---

## Liquidity
**Plain definition**  
How quickly and easily you can turn an asset into cash without losing much value.

**Common confusion**  
Assuming everything can be sold instantly at the current price.

**How it shows up in real life**  
Cash in your wallet is highly liquid. A house is much less liquid.

**Safety note**  
Keep enough liquid money for emergencies.

---

## Volatility
**Plain definition**  
How much the price of something moves up and down over short periods.

**Common confusion**  
Thinking high volatility always means high profit.

**How it shows up in real life**  
An asset that swings 10% in a single day is highly volatile.

**Safety note**  
High volatility can cause big losses even if the long-term trend is up.
